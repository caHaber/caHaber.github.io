My name is Casey Haber and I am Computer Scientist with a web-ecosystem emphasis. We must put an end to the traditional mindset surrounding web-dev and instead force the push for stable, simple, and powerful tools to democratize the complicated nature of the internet. My ultimate goal is to push boundaries as a web architect. Lets start focusing on collaboration, communication, and education to standardize learning and development of complex web based systems.


The majority of my time is spent experimenting on the web. My primary discipline is creating Data Visualizations and full-stack applications with node.js, d3.js and React (while also leveraging the systems and tools built into the web.) I am obsessed with the intersection of code, design and the communication of information. Data Visualization inspires exploration of knowledge in a methodologically rigorous manner. I spent years as a designer and although I enjoyed the processes involved, my passion was not fully present. My true passion is experimenting with the capabilities of the web in a attempt to democratize the skills and knowledge necessary to create cool stuff! I will be completing my Bachelors of Computer Science at the University of San Francisco in the Spring of 2018, with a Design minor already completed. Take a look at some of my React/D3 experiments!


I am a active member of the Visualization and Graphics Lab @ USF . I was flown out by USF last fall to present one of our accepted papers at the VINCI conference and have presented the paper many times beyond that. I worked as a Data Visualization intern at a small data-storytelling consulting firm last summer (Check out this interesting use of d3.force!) I worked as a Engineering and Design student employee for the Enterprise Systems & Applications division @ USF last year through this summer. Last spring, I worked as a teaching assistant for CS360 (Data Visualization) while simultaneously doing visualization and research work for a USF History professor’s upcoming book, titled Democratizing the Market: Place, Power & Politics in Early Philadelphia. Currently, I am the teaching assistant for CS315 Computer Architecture where we teach ARM v7 CPU implementation. This includes arm assembly programming, complete C emulation and digital design of the Armv7 instruction set. My name is Casey and I want everyone to think critically about their passions.
